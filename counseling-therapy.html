Our Approach

Customized Counseling for Your Needs

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a type of psychotherapy that aims to reduce symptoms of various mental health conditions, primarily depression and disorders such as post-traumatic stress disorder and anxiety disorders. CBT focuses on challenging unhelpful and irrational negative thoughts — referred to as "self-talk" — and replacing them with more rational positive self-talk, producing less anxiety and depression.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) emphasizes acceptance to deal with negative thoughts, feelings, symptoms, or circumstances while encouraging increased commitment to healthy, constructive activities that uphold one's values or goals.

Strategy: Socratic Questioning

A technique used in CBT that helps clients move beyond tunnel-vision thinking and consider new perspectives through logical, reflective questions. It supports clients in understanding negative core beliefs and distinguishing between thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.

📋

Implementation Tools

CBT uses a variety of tools including behavioral activation worksheets, cognitive restructuring exercises, automatic thought records, journaling, guided discovery, and exposure therapy techniques.
