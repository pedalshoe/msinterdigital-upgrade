The Mentoring Difference

Mentoring for Young People and Adults

Mentorship is to influence, guide, or direct a mentee — by teaching them, giving help and advice to a less experienced and often younger person. The mentor may also influence the mentee in both a personal and professional capacity.

The key difference between mentoring and coaching is that mentorship tends to focus on broader personal and professional development, whereas coaching often emphasizes specific tasks or objectives with more structured accountability.

"Establishing clear expectations early ensures a successful mentor–mentee relationship."

Strategy

Mentorship strategies include establishing trust and rapport through active listening, setting clear and achievable goals, providing constructive and honest feedback, and encouraging a growth mindset throughout the engagement.

🤝

Implementation

Tools include structured mentoring agreement templates covering meeting frequency, communication preferences, confidentiality, conflict resolution, goal setting, and career development. Clear expectations from the start ensure success.
